The End of the Stranger.

Privacy isn't about having a secret. It’s about controlling your own data. Meta glasses take that control away from everyone you look at.

We are currently sleepwalking into a world where "walking down the street" is a data-collection event. Meta’s Ray-Ban smart glasses are not just a fashion accessory; they are a distributed surveillance network that you didn't sign up for.

The Meta Threat Profile

1. Invisible Recording: The "recording light" on these glasses is a joke. It is easily obscured, taped over, or missed in daylight. It gives the public the illusion of transparency while providing none.

2. The AI Scrape: Meta is using the visual data from these glasses to train their AI. When a wearer looks at you, your face becomes training data for a private corporation. You cannot opt-out of someone else’s glasses.

3. Identifying Strangers: Using third-party apps, people are already using these glasses to find social media profiles and home addresses of strangers in real-time. This is doxxing as a service.

Why You Should Care

Think about where you go. A doctor’s office. A protest. A first date. A playground. In the past, you were protected by the fact that nobody knew who you were. You were a stranger, and that gave you freedom.

Smart glasses turn "looking" into "indexing." If a wearer looks at you, they aren't just seeing you; their device is processing you. This creates a chilling effect on society. People stop acting naturally when they feel they are being watched by every person in standard-looking eyewear.

The Action

We are demanding that the government step in before the concept of public anonymity is gone forever. This is not about banning tech; it is about Hardware-Level Accountability.
