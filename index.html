The Right to Remain a Stranger.

Technology should empower the wearer without diminishing the community. When smart glasses scan the world, the person across from you becomes data.

We are advocating for a balanced approach to wearable AI. As these devices become indistinguishable from standard eyewear, we believe it is essential to establish clear boundaries for the data they collect from those who haven't opted in.

Understanding the Impact

The following table outlines the current gap between device features and the privacy rights of the public.

Feature The Public Risk Proposed Solution
Always-On Audio Voice Command Wake Unintentional recording of private conversations in close proximity. Hardware mute switches.
AI Multimodal Object/Face Analysis Bystander faces are used to train AI models without notice or consent. Mandatory local-only processing.
Integrated Camera Discreet Recording The "recording LED" is often missed, removing the social cue of a camera. Physical camera shutters.
Facial Indexing Third-party Apps Linking your physical face to your home address or social media instantly. Ban on real-time doxxing APIs.

Taking Constructive Action

Policy change starts with communication. We invite you to copy this letter and send it to your local representatives to encourage a thoughtful legislative framework for wearable technology.

Manual Contact Template
SUBJECT: Request for Review: Privacy Frameworks for Wearable AI Dear Representative, I am writing as a constituent to share my thoughts on the need for updated privacy protections regarding wearable AI devices, such as smart glasses. While innovation is vital to our economy, current laws do not fully address the "always-on" nature of these cameras. Specifically, I am concerned about the non-consensual harvesting of bystander data and the loss of anonymity in our shared public spaces. I ask that your office considers supporting a framework based on: 1. Physical Transparency: Requiring devices to have visible, mechanical lens covers or shutters. 2. Bystander Sovereignty: Prohibiting the use of bystander imagery for AI training without an opt-in mechanism. 3. Real-Time Security: Restricting the integration of wearable devices with public facial recognition databases to prevent real-time doxxing. Thank you for your dedication to our community and for considering how we can build a future that respects both technology and privacy. Sincerely, [Your Name] [Your Address]
