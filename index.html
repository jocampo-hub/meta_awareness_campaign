Why This Campaign Exists


We are at a technological tipping point. Unlike smartphones, which must be held up to record, wearable AI captures data from a first-person perspective, often indistinguishable from standard eyewear.


The Privacy Shield Initiative was founded after researchers demonstrated "I-XRAY"—a system where smart glasses could identify strangers in real-time by cross-referencing their faces with public databases. Our mission is to advocate for hardware-level safeguards and updated consent laws.

The Technical Risks

Moving beyond "hidden recording" into the era of AI-enhanced surveillance.

Real-Time Doxxing

Using facial recognition APIs, a wearer can see your name, address, and social media profiles just by looking at you in a coffee shop.

Involuntary Data Harvesting

Everything the glasses see is often uploaded to cloud servers for "AI Training," meaning your private home interior or children's faces are now corporate data points.

The "Chilling Effect"

When people feel they are always being watched, they change how they speak, act, and protest, leading to a restricted, less free society.

Our Proposed "Shield Standards"

We are lobbying for these five fundamental requirements for all wearable tech:

01. Physical Indicators

A non-disableable, bright LED that must be lit whenever a camera or microphone is active.

02. Local Processing

Mandatory "Edge Computing" where facial data is never sent to a cloud server without explicit 3rd-party consent.

03. Sensitive Zone Geofencing

Automatic disabling of recording features in hospitals, locker rooms, and schools via encrypted location beacons.
