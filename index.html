Meta’s Ray-Ban smart glasses aren't just cameras; they are the front-end for a massive, multi-modal AI surveillance network. It is time to demand legislative guardrails.

The Privacy Threat Profile

While Meta markets these glasses as a way to "stay in the moment," the technical reality is the permanent erosion of the "Public Anonymity" we have enjoyed for centuries. Here is the detailed breakdown of the threat:

1. Multi-Modal AI & Real-Time Context

Unlike a standard camera, Meta glasses use "Look and Ask" AI. This means the device is constantly interpreting the world. It identifies people, brands, financial documents, and private conversations. This data isn't just captured—it is categorized by AI to build a 3D semantic map of your private life.

The "Stranger Doxxing" Paradox: Research has shown that smart glasses paired with facial recognition (like PimEyes or Clearview AI) can identify a stranger on the street and provide their home address, workplace, and social media in under 15 seconds.

2. The "Little Blue Light" Fallacy

Meta claims the LED recording indicator provides transparency. However, in bright daylight, this light is nearly invisible from more than five feet away. Furthermore, there is no hardware-level "kill switch," meaning the microphone can remain active for "Hey Meta" voice commands even when the camera is supposedly off.

3. Biometric Data Harvesting

Meta’s glasses collect "Metadata" that most users never see. This includes gait analysis (how you walk), head-movement patterns, and location history. When combined with Meta’s existing database (Facebook/Instagram), it creates a "Digital Twin" that can predict your behavior with frightening accuracy.
