Technology should bring us closer together without taking away our right to a private life. We believe it's time for a thoughtful conversation about smart glasses.

Smart glasses, like those developed by Meta, offer exciting possibilities for capturing memories. However, because they look just like regular eyewear, they change the dynamic of our public spaces. When we can't tell if we're being recorded, we tend to be less open, less authentic, and less connected to the people around us.

Why this matters to us

The Right to be Present: Privacy isn't about hiding; it's about the freedom to be yourself in public without worrying that your face is being analyzed by AI or stored on a corporate server forever.

Protecting the Vulnerable: In places like playgrounds, clinics, or community centers, the presence of invisible cameras can make people feel uneasy. We believe everyone deserves to feel safe and respected in these spaces.

Consent as a Standard: Currently, these devices can capture the data of hundreds of people in a single afternoon. We believe technology should prioritize the consent of the community, not just the wearer.

A Path Forward

We aren't against innovation. We are for responsible design. We're asking for simple changes, like clear physical indicators when a camera is active and better protections for the people who happen to be in the background of a photo or video.

How you can help

If you believe our privacy laws should keep up with new technology, please consider reaching out to your local representative. You can use the template below as a starting point for your letter.

Subject: Encouraging Thoughtful Regulation for Wearable AI Technology Dear Representative, I am writing to you today as a constituent who values both technological innovation and our community's right to privacy. I have been following the rise of smart glasses and wearable AI, such as Meta’s Ray-Ban glasses. While these devices offer new ways to connect, I am concerned about the lack of clear guidelines regarding public consent and data protection for bystanders. I would like to encourage your office to look into policy solutions that ensure: 1. Transparency: Requiring devices to have clear, unmistakable physical indicators when they are recording. 2. Protection: Ensuring that biometric data from bystanders is not collected or used to train AI models without their knowledge. 3. Respectful Design: Encouraging manufacturers to include features that respect "private zones" like schools and medical facilities. I believe that with the right safeguards, we can enjoy the benefits of new technology while still preserving the privacy that makes our society feel safe and open. Thank you for your time and for your dedication to our community. Sincerely, [Your Name] [Your Address]
