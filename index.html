The Real-Time Doxxing Threat

In 2024, it was proven that smart glasses can be paired with facial recognition to identify a stranger's personal details instantly. By 2026, this has moved from theory to a baseline capability. When a stranger looks at you, they aren't just seeing a person—they are accessing your digital footprint.

The Chilling Effect

Safe spaces—clinics, community centers, or private meetings—depend on the right to be a stranger. Meta glasses make surveillance indistinguishable from standard eyewear. This creates a world where people stop acting authentically because they assume they are always being indexed.

The Data Harvest

Meta isn't just selling hardware; they are mapping your world. Every hour these glasses are worn, Meta receives a first-person stream of human behavior. This data builds a "surveillance web" used to predict and manipulate consumer behavior through targeted AI suggestions.

The Right to be a Stranger

Privacy is the right to choose what you share. Smart glasses strip that choice away from everyone in the wearer's vicinity. We are advocating for hardware-level privacy as a legal requirement, not a corporate option.
