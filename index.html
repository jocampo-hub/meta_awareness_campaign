Smart Glasses Privacy Awareness Campaign

Understanding the risks, ethical concerns, and protections surrounding wearable recording technology

Issue Responsibility Concerns Solutions Awareness Resources

What Issue Are We Addressing?

The increasing use of smart glasses, including camera-enabled wearable devices, has raised concerns about individuals being recorded without their knowledge or consent in public and private spaces.

  • Hidden or subtle recording capabilities embedded in eyewear
  • People unknowingly being filmed in everyday environments
  • Data being stored, processed, or used for AI training
  • Potential misuse in academic, social, and professional settings

Who Is Responsible?

  • Users of smart glasses
  • Manufacturers such as Meta, Ray-Ban, and Oakley
  • Government and regulatory agencies allowing deployment

Key Concerns and Risks

Privacy Violations

  • Recording individuals without consent
  • Potential violations of HIPAA and FERPA in sensitive environments
  • Digital capture of private interactions

Data Storage & AI Use

  • Unclear storage practices for recorded footage
  • Possible use of data in AI training systems
  • Employees or third parties accessing sensitive content

Academic Dishonesty

  • Capturing exam content for later distribution
  • Using AI features to assist during tests
  • Undermining academic integrity policies

AI Stalking & Doxxing

  • Facial recognition linking identities to personal data
  • Exposure of private social media or home information
  • Increased risk of targeted harassment

Proposed Solutions

  • Geofencing restrictions in sensitive areas
  • Automatic face and object blurring in recordings
  • Mandatory visible indicators when recording is active
  • Auto-deletion of recordings after 24 hours
  • Clear transparency on data storage and usage
  • Institutional policies restricting use in exams or secure environments

How to Raise Awareness

  • Public service announcements explaining smart glasses capabilities
  • Educational websites outlining privacy rights
  • Campus and workplace awareness campaigns
  • Advocacy for stronger regulations and transparency laws

How to Protect Yourself

  • Understand your legal rights regarding recording in public
  • Learn to identify smart glasses and recording indicators
  • Avoid sensitive discussions in unsecured environments
  • Report suspicious recording activity when appropriate
