Your Privacy is In Plain Sight.

Meta's Ray-Ban smart glasses are turning bystanders into non-consenting subjects for AI training and facial recognition. It's time to understand the tech, recognize the risks, and protect your digital footprint.

Read The Report

The Surveillance Threat

Smart glasses are normalizing public surveillance. Here is what they don't put in the commercials.

👁️

Covert Recording

The tiny recording LED is designed to blend in. Users are actively bypassing it with tape, markers, or third-party hardware mods, allowing them to record private conversations and vulnerable moments with zero bystander awareness.

🧠

Secret AI Training

The 2026 Data Annotation Scandal revealed that unblurred POV footage—including views inside homes, bathrooms, and workplaces—was sent to third-party human contractors to train Meta's multimodal AI without explicit bystander consent.

👤

Facial Recognition Doxing

Third-party developers have already demonstrated real-time facial recognition pipelines. By looking at a stranger on the street, wearers can potentially surface a name, home address, and social media profiles in seconds.

Defective by Design

Not only are they invasive, but users report a staggering number of hardware failures and glitches.

Critical Hardware Flaws

  • 🔥
    Severe Overheating Frames reach uncomfortable temperatures during continuous Meta AI queries or extended video capture.
  • 🔋
    Phantom Battery Drain & Case Failures The glasses often fail to disconnect when placed in the case, draining the battery completely and failing to charge due to sensitive contact points.

Software Instability

  • 📶
    Random Connectivity Drops Constant Bluetooth drops that crash ongoing recordings or disrupt live audio playback, requiring frustrating factory resets.
  • 👆
    Unresponsive Touch Controls The temple touchpad frequently fails to register swipes or taps, particularly if the user is sweating or in humid environments.

Protect Your Public Privacy

Awareness is the first step. Learn how to spot smart glasses in public and demand legislation for mandated, unalterable recording indicators.
