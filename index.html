Smart Glasses Privacy Awareness Campaign

Addressing the risks of wearable recording technology, AI surveillance concerns, and the need for stronger privacy protections.

Issue Responsibility Risks Solutions Awareness Resources

What Issue Are We Addressing?

Smart glasses with built-in cameras can record people without their knowledge or consent, raising serious privacy concerns in public, academic, and professional environments.

  • Hidden or subtle recording of individuals
  • Unclear data storage and AI usage policies
  • Potential misuse in schools, workplaces, and public spaces

Who Is Responsible?

  • Device users who record without consent
  • Companies such as Meta, Ray-Ban, and Oakley
  • Regulators allowing deployment without strict safeguards

Key Risks and Concerns

Privacy Violations

  • Recording people without consent
  • Potential HIPAA and FERPA violations
  • Loss of control over personal image

Data & AI Misuse

  • Unknown storage of recordings
  • Possible AI training on user data
  • Employee or third-party access risks

Academic Dishonesty

  • Capturing exam materials secretly
  • Using AI assistance during tests
  • Undermining academic integrity

AI Surveillance Risks

  • Facial recognition linking identities
  • Exposure of personal information
  • Increased stalking and doxxing risk

Proposed Solutions

  • Geofencing in sensitive areas (schools, hospitals, exams)
  • Mandatory recording indicators (LED or alerts)
  • Auto-blurring faces and sensitive objects
  • Automatic deletion after 24 hours
  • Clear disclosure of data usage policies

Raising Awareness

  • Public awareness campaigns about smart glasses risks
  • Educational websites explaining user rights
  • Campus and workplace policy updates
  • Advocacy for stronger privacy legislation
